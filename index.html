C390 Portfolio Development module helps you prepare a showcase of what you have learnt in Diploma in Mobile Software Development and the web and mobile apps that you had created.

This is a 2-semester module and you will be taking it in your year 2 sem 2 and year 3 sem 1.

Every student will be assigned a supervisor to provide guidance and mentoring.

Each student must put up at least 4 projects but you should aim for more for a more favorable portfolio to showcase to your prospective employers.

You are expected to complete at least 3 projects in year 2 sem 2 so that you can better focus in your Final Year Project in year 3 sem 1.

You will be graded on both the process and the final deliverable of the projects.

Specifically,

  1. Create a blog site for portfolio
  2. On your own blog site, create a blog post for every project. You must have one blog post for each project.
    Each post should be updated with a proposal, storyboard and competitive analysis of the project.
    Take a look at your seniors' work at Exemplary Portfolios. These portfolios serve as a guide and but you are encouraged to surpass them.
  3. Competitive Analysis should have the following
  • 2 competitors at least
  • Competitors product name and URL to their apps/website
  • Competitors' features and gaps
  • SWOT

The process and deliverable are graded on 4 components with their weight as shown below.

Technical – 30%

Concept and Approach – 30%

Creativity and Uniqueness – 30%

Presentation – 10%

Source of projects:

  • Daily Problem
  • FYP Related
  • Creativity
  • Hackathon
  • Competition
  • Baseline Proposals

You should have a clear idea of of the different milestones so that you do not fall behind the schedule. There will be supplementary talks to help to equip you with building your portfolio. The dates of the talks are in the timetable. You can access the materials used in briefing and talks here.

We have two labs that you can use to work on your portfolio. They are Samsung Lab at E63A (available every school day) and iOS Lab at E63B (available on day 3 and day 4). Please approach your supervisor on arrangement to access the labs.

A good portfolio would be very useful for you to get noticed as an IT professional. Wonder how the life of a software engineer is like? Check this out: Day in the Life: Software Engineer.
